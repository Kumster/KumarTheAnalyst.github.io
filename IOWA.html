This project analyzes Iowa liquor sales using public datasets to identify patterns, trends, and risk indicators in alcohol sales across regions and seasons. The notebook includes descriptive statistics, visualizations, and return distribution comparisons against a normal curve to evaluate volatility.

Key areas covered in the project:

  • Data cleaning & aggregation of liquor sales by store and city
  • Seasonality analysis of high-volume periods (e.g. holidays)
  • Comparison of actual return distribution to normal distribution (fat tails)
  • Risk and performance profiling using Python visualizations

Technologies used include Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn. This project showcases statistical exploration and visualization skills applicable to public health, business intelligence, and regulatory domains.
